How it Works

1. Send one of your emails to Kwa@sant.com

2. One of our Bookers will read the thread and build a meeting invitation

3. The meeting invitation gets sent back to you and your attendees

4. Add it to your calendar with a single click






Kwasant

It just takes one step..

Details

We take the emails you provide to us and put them in front of of our human Booking Agents, who do what a personal assistant would do: they read the message, extract the key elements about the meeting, and enter them into a calendar.

This generates a Meeting Invitation that we email back to you using the open “ICS” standard. If you're using common calendaring software like Google Calendar, Apple iCal, and Microsoft Outlook, these emails show up in your inbox and allow you to add them to your calendar with one click. Think of us as your personal assistant. If we can read your email and figure out the meeting time, we’ll do it. You don’t have to give the information to us in any special format. If the information you send us is too ambiguous, we’ll send you back a request for clarification. We delete all email we receive 7 days after the event is scheduled.

FAQ

Just what problem are you solving here?

The internet offers fantastic services these days, but it’s still an annoying task to convert conversations into useful meeting requests. Some people have personal assistants to help with this, but most of us don’t. Now, you can get personal assistant help when you need it.

How do you make money?

Someday, we’re probably going to charge a small monthly fee for booking services. But everyone using BookIt now will get at least one free year of service.

What email addresses do you use?

We send your meeting request right back to the address you sent your information from. However, you’ll soon be able to specify which address to use.

What else do you do?

We're working on adding a bunch of additional ways you can save time by tapping into our web assistants.

Is this all you do?

Well, we’re pretty new, but we have a big list of services we’re adding. If you have suggestions or feature requests, check out our forums.

Do you have a calendar I can view?

We’re not a replacement for your existing calendar software, although we’re always interested in knowing where you’d like it to be better. Maybe we’ll write a calendaring solution that you can use.

Can I just “CC” you on an email?

Yes. Just add now@bookit.cc to your addressee list. You can also “Bcc” us.

What is ICS, anyway?

ICS stands for Internet Calendar Software. It’s an agreed-upon open internet standard for specifying the details of an event in a text file. When your email system receives an email that has one of these ICS files attached to it, it hands it to your calendaring software, which converts it into a full-fledged event request that you can add to your calendar with one click.

What if I want to change my event?

You’ve got two choices. You can send the changes to us at now@bookit.cc, or you can double-click on the calendar event, after adding it to your calendar, and make the change yourself.

How do you decide who else to send the meeting request to?

Our job as your PAW is to save you time and increase your productivity. If we can figure out who you’re having the meeting with, we’ll send the meeting request to them to on behalf of you.

How much information do I need to email to you?

Most users just copy us on their final confirmation email to their counterpart. You can edit down your messages before you send them to us. Just try to leave in information about location, time and attendees, so we don’t have to bother you asking for clarifications

Do you call restaurants for reservations? Can you buy tickets for me? Do you __________ the __________ for me?

Not yet. But we’re very interested in knowing which activities you’d like personal assistance with. Vote on your feature requests here.

What languages do you support? Right now our PA’s are all English readers and writers. But we’ll soon be adding other languages. Vote on the language you want.

Who is your mascot?

Our mascot is Maginot the Cat. He’s 20 pounds and very fluffy

Can you also send a meeting invite to the other attendees?

Yes, they'll get one too.

What if I don't give you enough detail about the meeting? Do you just guess?

In general, we'll book the information we're sure about, and if necessary, email you for clarification.

Do you have a mascot?
